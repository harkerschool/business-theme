Business & Entrepreneurship

Hosted by students, powered by ideas. Educating, inspiring, changing the world.

Like a booming startup, Harker’s student-led business & entrepreneurship (B.E.) initiative has rapidly expanded to offer a variety of inspiring programs since its launch in 2013.

Harker’s B.E. department aims to develop, foster and provide business and entrepreneurial education, leadership opportunities and career preparatory programs to upper school students.

DECA

An international student and business competition association which:

  • Prepares high school and college students for leadership in marketing, finance, hospitality and management.
  • Promotes understanding and appreciation of the free enterprise system, and the responsibilities of citizenship.

Participants attend year-round conferences and competitions across the U.S.

CareerConnect

Harker CareerConnect is a student-led career pre-paratory, networking and professionalism program.

We connect students to their futures through mean-ingful mentorships, provocative panel discussions, and live hands-on programs with Silicon Valley’s leaders.

Business and entrepreneurship concepts touch all industries, and we dig deep into issues that matter to students.

Mentorship Program

CareerConnect mentorship offers sophomores and juniors the opportunity to participate in a rewarding, multi-faceted working relationship.

Mentors show mentees what it’s like to work in a field, give them an overall experience of the industry, and advise them on a future in it. Fields include Medicine, Business, Computer Science/Engineering, and Law.

April 15 — Kick-off Banquet
Mentors and mentees will meet in person and devise a game plan.

In the following months, mentors will show their mentees their work places, meet in person over a smoothy (students love treats) to offer guidance, and then over email and the phone. The goal is to “connect” in one form or another at least once a month.

We’re looking for more mentors! Contact us at CareerConnect@harker.org if you are up for a rewarding mentor experience, or if you have a recommendation for a great mentor.

CareerConnect Professionalism

To prepare students for their professional futures, we present workshops that teach them how to:

  • Develop networking skills
  • Build a resume
  • Create a LinkedIn profile
  • Dress professionally
  • And other pertinent skills

Events are held throughout Silicon Valley, from the Computer History Museum to Stanford University. Students gain firsthand experience networking with professionals—and they participate in industry events that few teens are even aware of.

We’re looking for more professional participants and opportunities! Contact us at CareerConnect@harker.org.

B.E. Courses

We’re thrilled to announce the launch of our brand-new business curriculum with two debut courses:

  • Principles of Business and Entrepreneurship—fall semester
  • Advanced Fundamentals of Business and Entrepreneurship—spring semester

Designed as a “mini MBA” each semester consists of four modules—marketing, accounting and finance, entrepreneurship, and strategy and management.

The first semester delivers a broad-stroke approach, providing essential concepts and principles that are brought into action through a capstone project. The second semester delves deeper into the principles and concepts previously learned, through research and discussion of Harvard case studies, with various team/class projects interwoven throughout.

Students will “learn by doing” as they step into roles of CEO, CFO, EVP —and more— to bring solutions to the complex and extraordinary challenges that business leaders face every day.

Entrepreneur in the Classroom — Summer 2014

We are partnering with The Wharton School to bring an intensive, certificate-based program will help students take flight into the business world.

The program weaves together concurrent summer programs to create an immersive experience that will help shape the next generation of business leaders. Students will learn about financial literacy, business principles, business plans, and how to grow and develop their own endeavors.

Still in development, plans for the program include mentorships, entrepreneurs-in-residence, special guest lecturers, and participation with our DECA chapter and incubator programs to offer a full experience.

International Entrepreneurial Exchange/Expedition — Summer 2015

The IEE/E is an exciting opportunity to explore developing markets around the globe.

Meet international entrepreneurs, tour businesses and factories, have one-on-one meetings with local and influential leaders and incubators, and learn from many other eye-opening business and entrepreneurship-related experiences.

For Summer 2015 we’ve penciled in London, but the final destination depends on student demand and logistical factors.
